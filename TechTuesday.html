These devices are tangible and can be almost anything imaginable such as ordinary household appliances ambient to a microwave or fridges which are implanted with sensors and software in that family to sophisticated industrial tools and things of that nature. Throughout the ongoing years, IoT has evolved into one of the rare technologies that hold an immense level of significance in the 21 century. Unlocking the threshold to connect to simple, day to day objects -kitchen appliances, cars, thermostats, baby monitors, etc- to the internet's network, seamless communication has become a reality between humans, processes, and things of that nature. By the means of low-cost computing, the cloud, big data, analytics, and mobile technologies, all types of physical objects can distribute, and receive data information with close to none human interaction within the system. This highly concentrated hyperconnected world, opens many different pathways for digital systems to record, monitor, and tweak each interaction between other uniquely connected things.
